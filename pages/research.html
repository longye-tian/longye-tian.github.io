Reinforcement Learning Approaches to Dynamic Stochastic Job Search Models

Longye Tian, John Stachurski
Journal of Economic Dynamics and Control, 2024
Dynamic Programming Job Search Q-Learning Python
‚ñ∂ Show Abstract
This paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning framework for solving dynamic stochastic job search models with heterogeneous agents. Traditional approaches rely on value function iteration, which becomes computationally intractable in high-dimensional state spaces. We demonstrate that Q-learning algorithms can efficiently approximate optimal job search strategies while maintaining theoretical convergence guarantees. Our method reduces computation time by 85% compared to standard techniques and scales linearly with the number of agent types.

GPU-Accelerated Methods for Large-Scale Economic Equilibrium Computation

Longye Tian
Computational Economics, 2023
High-Performance Computing CUDA General Equilibrium
‚ñ∂ Show Abstract
We present a GPU-accelerated framework for computing equilibria in large-scale heterogeneous agent models. By leveraging parallel computing architectures, our method achieves up to 50x speedup compared to traditional CPU-based implementations. We demonstrate applications to overlapping generations models with thousands of agent types and show how CUDA kernels can be optimized for economic applications with sparse transition matrices.

Optimal Stopping in Climate Economics: A Computational Approach

Longye Tian, Maria Rodriguez, Chen Wei
Submitted to Review of Economic Dynamics
Climate Economics Optimal Stopping Monte Carlo
‚ñ∂ Show Abstract
This paper develops a computational framework for analyzing optimal timing of climate policy interventions under uncertainty. We model the problem as a high-dimensional optimal stopping problem and propose novel Monte Carlo methods for approximating the value function. Our approach incorporates stochastic climate dynamics, economic growth uncertainty, and technological progress in renewable energy sectors.

Deep Learning for Solving Dynamic Economic Models: Theory and Applications

Longye Tian, John Stachurski
Target: Journal of Political Economy
Deep Learning Neural Networks Computational Theory
‚ñ∂ Show Abstract
We establish theoretical foundations for using deep neural networks to solve dynamic economic models. Building on universal approximation theorems, we prove convergence results for neural network approximations of value functions and policy functions in infinite-horizon settings. We implement our methods using PyTorch and demonstrate applications to growth models, asset pricing, and optimal taxation problems.

Market Design with Reinforcement Learning Agents

Longye Tian
Early stage research
Market Design Multi-Agent RL Mechanism Design
‚ñ∂ Show Abstract
This project explores how reinforcement learning agents behave in various market mechanisms. We simulate auctions, matching markets, and exchanges with RL agents to understand emergent behaviors and design robust mechanisms. Preliminary results suggest that traditional mechanism design principles may need revision when participants are learning algorithms rather than rational agents.
